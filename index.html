How the count works ›

For most routes (Skilled Worker, Health & Care, Spouse/Partner, Global Talent, Ancestry, Innovator Founder) the rule is: no more than 180 days outside the UK in any rolling 12-month period across your qualifying residence — assessed since 11 Jan 2018 on a rolling basis, not by calendar year.

The big gauge shows your worst 12-month window — that's the one that can get an application refused. The "current window" tile shows your last 365 days from today, so you can plan upcoming trips.

A single continuous absence of more than ~6 months can break continuous residence entirely and reset the clock — this app flags that for you.

Different routes vary (e.g. Long Residence uses 10 years), and some absences may be exempt. The 5-year route remained in force in mid-2026, but rules can change. Always check the live guidance at gov.uk.

A personal tracking aid, not legal advice. The Home Office's own record of your travel is what ultimately counts — keep your passport stamps and boarding passes. Verify your specific route on gov.uk or with a registered adviser.
